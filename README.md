# SegLab — Open-Source Brain MRI Lesion Segmentation and Neuroanatomy Training

SegLab is an open-source brain MRI lesion segmentation and neuroanatomy training platform built for education, research, and clinical training. It allows users to load T1-weighted MRI cases, annotate lesions interactively, compare their masks against expert reference masks, and receive immediate quantitative feedback through Dice and IoU. Instead of treating segmentation as a passive viewing task, SegLab turns it into a measurable training workflow.

---

## Why SegLab

Most medical imaging tools are built either for research pipelines or for clinical viewing. Very few are designed to help people *learn* lesion segmentation in a structured, objective way.

SegLab was created to fill that gap.

It is designed for students, residents, researchers, and clinicians who want to train lesion recognition and segmentation on brain MRI while receiving direct, reproducible feedback on performance. The goal is simple: make segmentation practice more interactive, more quantitative, and more useful.

---

## Core Features

- **Interactive lesion annotation on brain MRI**
- **Case-based workflow** for loading and reviewing individual studies
- **Reference-mask comparison** against expert or gold-standard segmentations
- **Instant quantitative scoring** with Dice and IoU
- **Attempt-level feedback** to support structured learning and repeated practice
- **Open-source codebase** that can be extended for teaching, research, or lab-specific workflows

---

## What SegLab is for

SegLab is especially useful for:

- **Neuroanatomy and neuroradiology teaching**
- **Stroke, tumor, and lesion-segmentation training**
- **Segmentation benchmarking in educational settings**
- **Research projects involving manual annotation practice**
- **Building structured teaching datasets and training exercises**

It is not just a viewer and not just an annotation utility. It is a training-oriented segmentation environment.
